TK Like a Pop Quiz or Something

  1. What are the coordinates of the vertex of parabola that is the graph of the function \(g(x) = x^2-14x+33\,?\)
  2. What are the roots of the quadratic polynomial \(x^2-14x+33\,?\)
  3. What are the roots of the quadratic polynomial \(2x^2+3x-4\,?\) Express the values of the roots as decimal numbers rounded to three figures to the right of the decimal.
  4. What must the value of \(\omega\) be if \[0.628\bigl(34\omega - 957\bigr)^{1.61} = 72062.66\,?\]
  5. What is a formula for an example of a quadratic polynomial function that has no real roots, and whose graph is a parabola with vertex located at \((3,-4)?\)
  6. What is a formula for the quadratic function whose graph passes through these three points? \[(0,-18) \quad (6,0) \quad (-9,0) \]
  7. What are the exact coordinates of the point(s) where these two curves intersect? \[ y = x^2-x-14 \qquad \qquad y = 6-2x \]

  8. Extra

    Imagine that you have a can of red paint, a can of blue paint, and a large supply of wooden cubes, all the same size. You decide to paint the cubes by making each face either solid red or solid blue. For example, you might paint one cube all red. The next cube you may decide to give three red faces and three blue faces. Perhaps the third cube can also be given three red and three blue faces, but painted in such a way that it doesn't match the second cube. How many cubes can you paint in this manner that will be different from each other?

    Two cubes are considered alike if one can be turned so that all its sides match the corresponding sides of the other cube.
