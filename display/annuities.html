Annuities & Loans

  1. Suppose Anita wants to save up for a down payment on a home. She opens an annuity account with her bank that offers 3% annual interest compounded monthly, and she plans to deposit $800 per month.
    1. How much will she have saved for a down payment after five years?
    2. How much would she had saved if she instead stashed $800 into a coffee can burried in her backyard each month?
  2. Over the course of his life, Billiam has saved up $1.2 million in a retirement account that yields 2.5% annual interest. He’s 67 now, and ready to retire.
    1. If Billiam anticipates living to be 100-years-old and wants his saving to last that long, how much can he afford to withdraw each month?
    2. Billiam decides that’s not enough money per month to support his extravagant lifestyle, so he withdraws $7000 per month in his retirement instead. How old will Billiam be when he runs out of money?
    3. Billiam finds this unacceptable! He is convinced he’ll live to be 100-years-old, and he will settle for no less than $7000 per month in his retirement. The only option then is to defer his retirement and keep working. If Billiam can contribute $2,200 per month into his retirement account while he’s working, when can he finally retire?
