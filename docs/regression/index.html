Math113 | Regression Project

Now that we have talked about linear regression, we’ve reached a point of mathematical sophistication that we can apply what we’ve learned. Do some research and find some data online with a numerical independent variable (so your horizontal axis can be thought of as ascending from left to right) that is relevant to your interests or your major. If you don’t know where to look for such data, this page could provide a good starting point:

  • G2: 50 Best Open Data Sources […]

Once you find this data, copy it into Excel, plot the data (select the data, then select “Insert” → “Charts” → “Scatter”), and fit a linear model to the data (select the scatter plot points, then select “Chart Design” → “Add Chart Element” → “Trendline” → “Linear”). Excel calls this regression line a trendline. For the deliverable, next class meeting I’d like you to give me a printed copy of your Excel plot marked up with some details and analysis:

  1. Title your plot and label the axes; it should be clear to me, or to anyone looking at your plot really, what the plot is illustrating.
  2. Label your trendline with its formula in Excel: double-click trendline, then select “Display Equation on Chart”.
  3. Same something smart: write a brief analysis of your data. At the very least the formula for the trendline tells you if the data is increasing or decreasing overall. What does the slope of the formula for you model mean in the context of your data? This can be done in Excel, or post-print by hand.
  4. (Optional) Customize the design of your plot in Excel; presentation is important.
  5. (Optional) Play around with different kinds of models that Excel provides, and see if any of them appear to fit your data better than the linear model. We’ll be talking about some of these models later in the course.

My hope is that by keeping this project simple, with only a few requirements, you have time to really dig-in to researching data online that interests you, and time to play in Excel to discover its capabilities.
