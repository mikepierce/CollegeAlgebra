Logarithmic Functions

  1. Write down an explanation of how you can estimate the value of \(\log_{10}(31415926)\) without appealing to technology.
  2. Without appealing to technology, calculate the exact value of the following logarithms \[ \log_3(81) \qquad \qquad \qquad \log_2(1024) \qquad \qquad \qquad \log_5(3125) \qquad \qquad \qquad \ln(\mathrm{e}^6) \]
  3. Using a calculator, write down decimal expressions of the following numbers rounded to two decimal places. \[ \log_{10}(31415926) \qquad \qquad \qquad \ln(100) \qquad \qquad \qquad \log_5(43) \]
  4. Find the value(s) of \(x\) that satisfies each of these equations. \[ 2^x = 2048 \qquad \qquad \qquad 3^x = 6000 \qquad \qquad \qquad 3^x2^x = 1234 \qquad \qquad \qquad \mathrm{e}^x = 42 \]
  5. If \(\log_7(x) = 5,\) what must the exact value of the following expressions be? (Hint: this is an exercise of your effective use of the “rules of logarithms”.) \[ \log_7\!\left(\frac{x^3}{7}\right) \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \log_7\!\left(49x^2\right) \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \log_7\!\left(\frac{\sqrt{x}}{49}\right) \]

  6. Rewrite each of the following expressions as a single logarithm. I.e. “simplify” these into a form that looks like \(\log_b(\text{stuff})\) for some \(\text{stuff}.\)

    \(\displaystyle \log_7(x) + 4\log_7(y) \)
    \(\displaystyle \ln(5x) - 3\ln(z) \)
    \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{2}\log_5(4x) + 2\log_5(x+1) \)
  7. In May 2008 an earthquake of magnitude 6.8 struck Honshu, Japan, causing some injuries and building damage. In March 2011 a magnitude 9.0 earthquake stuck Honshu, killing thousands of people. How much more powerful was this second earthquake?
